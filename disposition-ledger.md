# disposition-ledger

[![Quillx](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/qainsights/Quillx/main/badges/quillx-4.svg)](https://github.com/qainsights/Quillx) Generated by Claude under human direction. The gate rule, the ledger format, and the worked example were defined by a human and refined through production use.

Review findings from an audit agent or `/code-review` are worthless the moment they are read and not acted on. This skill keeps a file-backed ledger so every blocker or major finding reaches exactly one terminal state, accepted, rejected, deferred, or blocked, before the work it concerns is called done.

## What it does

Domain: review-finding lifecycle tracking. Reader: whoever runs an audit agent (plan-auditor, code-auditor, pr-reviewer) or signs off on the branch the findings concern.

It is distinct from `superpowers:receiving-code-review`, which governs how to evaluate one piece of feedback: verify it against the codebase, push back with reasoning, or implement it. This skill governs whether the whole set is resolved. It doesn't replace that judgement, it records the outcome and gates on completeness, which is what catches a finding that gets silently marked accepted on a glance, or lost entirely when a review session is interrupted mid-way.

It plugs in at two points: adjudication, after an audit but before a branch starts, where every blocker or major finding gets a row that the branch's own spec can then cite; and before the branch is called done, alongside `superpowers:verification-before-completion` and `superpowers:finishing-a-development-branch`, where the ledger is reopened to confirm no blocker or major row is blank and every deferred row names an approver. A green test suite does not close a ledger that still has a blank row in it.

## The gate

Every blocker, major, critical, or high finding gets exactly one of:

- **accepted**: will be fixed; the row links to the commit, task, or fix location that resolves it.
- **rejected**: will not be actioned; the row cites the specific code, test, or fact that makes the finding wrong. A bare "won't fix" does not satisfy the gate.
- **deferred**: postponed, with a named human approver and a condition that reopens it. A model cannot self-approve a deferral, only propose one. A proposed deferral is marked `deferred (PENDING <name>)` so it still reads as an open row until sign-off lands. Deferring an irreversible change (a dropped column, a migration, a deletion) needs a reopen condition that fires before the destructive step runs, not after it.
- **blocked (needs <what>)**: the finding can't be verified from the evidence at hand. This is the pressure valve against both extremes: marking something accepted on a glance, and rabbit-holing on one finding while the rest of the ledger goes untouched.

Bulk dispositioning ("accepting all 6", "rejecting the whole batch, these are all fine") is the gate failing quietly under a different name. Evidence and approval are per finding; a batch note satisfies neither.

## Ledger format

A markdown table in a tracked file (`docs/disposition-ledger.md`, or a per-branch `REVIEW.md`), one row per finding: ID, Source, Severity, Finding, Disposition, Evidence/Link, Approved by. Markdown rather than JSON: it's greppable and diffable in the same PR the findings concern, needs no parser, and sits next to the plan or branch it tracks.

IDs follow `<source-agent>-<n>`, sequential per source. Severity and Finding copy the auditor's own wording verbatim rather than being paraphrased when transcribed. Rows are written the moment a finding is dispositioned, not batched to the end of a session, so an interrupted review leaves a partial ledger on disk rather than an empty one.
