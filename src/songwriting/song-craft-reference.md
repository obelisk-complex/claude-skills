# Song-craft reference

Practical craft for writing and structuring songs. Companion to `SKILL.md` and `his-signature.md`. Fuller sourcing and worked rewrites live in llm-wiki `sources/songwriting-craft-and-ai-lyric-tells.md`.

## 1. Song form: what each section does
- **Intro:** establish tempo, key/mode, and sonic palette before any narrative lands.
- **Verse:** delivers new narrative information; lyric content changes each pass even when the melody repeats.
- **Pre-chorus:** transitional; builds anticipation and hints at the chorus payoff, usually on a progression that wants to resolve forward.
- **Chorus:** the emotional core and central repeated statement; the release of tension the verse and pre-chorus built.
- **Bridge / middle-8:** breaks the pattern the listener internalised by chorus 2, so the final chorus feels like release, not repeat. It must do exactly one job: a perspective flip, a stakes-raise, or a reveal that recontextualises what came before. A bridge that just restates the chorus has not earned its place.
- **Drop** (dance-music chorus-equivalent): the moment withheld low end and full beat return; the arrival the build-up existed to earn.
- **Breakdown:** strips the kick and heavy percussion, foregrounds pads/melody/vocal, re-presents the hook in a stripped, intimate texture.
- **Outro:** winds energy down or cuts hard; a deliberate exit, not a trail-off.
- **Escalate each pass:** more instrumentation, a higher vocal take, one changed lyric detail, so the post-bridge final chorus reads as a peak, not a loop.

## 2. Prosody and singability
- Match stressed syllables to strong beats. Accentual stress beats raw syllable count; a "correct" count can still scan badly if the stress falls wrong.
- Front-heavy lines (stress on beat 1) read as settled and resolved; back-heavy lines (stress set back in the bar) read as unstable and searching. Choose per section.
- Keep sung lines roughly 6 to 10 syllables by default. Denser 10 to 13+ syllable lines are a legitimate contrast tool, not a default.
- Contrast density by section: a packed verse against an open, airy chorus (or the reverse) so each section is distinguishable by feel alone.
- Leave a real gap (held note, rest, turnaround) at phrase-end for a breath. A line that runs straight into the next is unsingable regardless of scan.
- Put the most important line right before a musical gap so it has room to land.

## 3. Rhyme craft (beyond perfect end-rhyme)
- Vary type on purpose: perfect rhyme for the chorus's most stable, declarative line; assonance, consonance, and slant elsewhere to avoid sing-song. Slant reads as instability against perfect rhyme's stability.
- The wider family: end, internal, slant, plus broken, trailing, apocopated, weak/light, linked, and echo rhyme. (Variant definitions are directional summaries, not primary-source-confirmed.)
- Rigid four-bar perfect end-rhyme on every line is a known AI tell (nursery-rhyme cadence). Avoid it as a default pattern.

## 4. Hooks, motifs, repetition, call-and-response
- Hook: short, catchy, repeated identically every chorus pass so the ear locks on.
- Motif: a short melodic or lyrical shape that can return varied across sections to unify the whole song, not only verbatim in the chorus.
- Call-and-response: write a phrase as a musical question and answer it in the next phrase, or leave an explicit gap in the hook for an answering voice.
- Setup and payoff: plant an expectation early (a lyric detail, a melodic shape, an unresolved question); its later return should read as earned, not coincidence.

## 5. Melody and harmony for a lyricist
- Major reads bright and resolved; minor reads dark and melancholic; same contour, different charge by key.
- Modes shade finer: Dorian is minor but lifted; Mixolydian is major but bluesy and unresolved; Lydian is bright, floating, transcendent. Useful when plain minor is too heavy or plain major too flat.
- A strong cadence (V-I) reads as arrival and completion; a deliberately weak or avoided cadence leaves incompleteness, good for a verse that should feel unresolved into the chorus.
- A key change (commonly before the final chorus) is a well-worn lift. Pair it with a lyric moment that should feel like a genuine step up in stakes, not decoration.

## 6. Build-and-drop / rave-style arrangement (traits only, no artist or track names)
- Overall shape: intro -> breakdown or verse-equivalent (pads, sparse melody/vocal) -> build-up (layers elements, withholds low end) -> drop (full kick, bass, and lead land together).
- The build-up mechanism is subtractive: filter or mute the sub-bass through the build so its return at the drop lands disproportionately hard.
- Snare rolls accelerate (quarter -> eighth -> sixteenth notes), often two layered snares with rising velocity, for physical forward momentum into the drop.
- Risers: a sustained note or noise with rising pitch and/or filter sweep (commonly white noise, low to high). Their job is pure anticipation, independent of rhythm.
- Breakdown precedes the build in the trance/rave family: kick and heavy percussion drop out, pads and the main hook come forward, an intimate lift before the next build.
- Fast, euphoric, high-BPM rave-adjacent styles as a trait cluster: anthemic bright synth leads over a distorted, punchy kick; long emotionally open breakdowns (pads/piano/vocal); builds stacked in risers and snare rolls; a drop where kick and lead melody move together; off-beat open hi-hats and rolling basslines at the harder end.

## 7. AI-lyric tells and humanising counter-moves
- Cut on sight: whispers, echoes, shadows, tapestry, neon lights, "gentle"/"softly" as reflex modifiers; and the cliche phrase bank (shattered dreams, endless night, fading light, waves crashing, unseen tears, lost in time, forgotten memories, burning bridges, broken chains, empty streets).
- Avoid rigid four-bar perfect end-rhyme on every line (nursery-rhyme cadence, no syncopation).
- Avoid relentless, frictionless positivity. Let one verse carry a flaw or doubt; total uplift with nothing at stake reads as machine output.
- Counter-move: object-write the real memory first (about ten minutes, senses only, no feeling-words), then pull the concrete detail from that rather than inventing an abstraction.
- Counter-move: replace an over-explained emotion ("I feel so alone") with one camera-testable, sense-bound detail specific to the scene.
